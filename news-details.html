Yoruba Culture Thrives in Modern Times

By Adetola Adeyemi | March 12, 2025

In recent years, Yoruba culture has witnessed a resurgence among younger generations eager to reconnect with their roots. Across Nigeria and the diaspora, Yoruba language classes, art exhibitions, and festivals are gaining popularity once again.

At the heart of this revival are cultural ambassadors and institutions promoting Yoruba heritage through media, fashion, and film. According to historian Chief Adekunle Ogundipe, “Our stories are finding new life through technology. From online platforms to animated tales, Yoruba identity is stronger than ever.”

Experts believe that this renewed interest is essential for preserving traditional knowledge systems and moral values. From the popular Olojo Festival in Ile-Ife to Yoruba-themed movies on OpenTvplus, there’s a growing movement towards cultural preservation and pride.

“Our culture is our crown — it must shine, not fade,” said cultural advocate and artist, Ayo Alade.

As digital storytelling platforms expand, the Yoruba people are proving that modernity and tradition can thrive side by side. OpenTvplus continues to play a vital role in showcasing these stories through its programs, documentaries, and creative projects.
